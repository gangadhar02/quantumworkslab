Overview

The importance of AI to robotic’s future

AI is at the forefront of robotics innovation, driving the development of intelligent systems capable of interacting with the physical world. From teleoperation to achieving AGI-level intelligence, training frontier models with diverse, high-quality data is critical to mastering dexterous manipulation, navigating complex environments, or bridging simulation-to-reality gaps.

Challenges

Navigating robotics’ complexities for AI

Building AI models for robotics—especially teleoperation and real-world adaptability—presents unique challenges. Diverse physical constraints, novel hardware configurations, and dynamic environments demand highly skilled operators that can capture high-utility data from complex environments. Success also depends on a scalable platform, expert human oversight, and continuous refinement.

Robotics use case dependent on high-quality data

Dexterous manipulation & object interaction

Label object poses, grasp success/failure, and contact points to train robots for precise, human-like handling.

Vision-based navigation & scene understanding

Sim-to-real transfer learning

Teleoperation refinement
